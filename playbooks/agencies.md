# SBL Playbook — Agencies & Lead Gen Teams

*42,000+ delivered messages · 496 campaigns*

## Who this is for
Digital marketing agencies, lead gen agencies, outbound sales agencies. Running LinkedIn outreach at client scale — multiple accounts, multiple ICPs, zero ban risk.

---

## Core insight
Agencies have the highest hot lead rate of any vertical — 37.9%. They are operationally aware buyers. They don't care about "AI-powered outreach." They care about: running 10 client campaigns simultaneously without accounts getting flagged, keeping client conversations separated, scaling volume without hiring, white-labelling the infrastructure.

**The angle is operations, not features.**

---

## Campaign types (ranked by quality)

### 1. Competitor and Signal-Based Outreach
Target people engaging with competitor agency content or tools (Dripify, HeyReach, Expandi, Apollo).

| Metric | Benchmark |
|--------|-----------|
| CR acceptance | 25–40% |
| Reply rate | 12–20% |
| Hot leads per 100 delivered | 8–15 |
| Avg conversation depth | 4–6 messages |

**Lead sources:**
- Competitor posts: SBL Post Likes or Comment to DM on competitor agency tool posts
- Comment to DM: posts about outbound strategy, SDR scaling, lead gen ops
- Community signal: agency owner communities, sales leader groups, growth operator networks
- Sales Navigator keywords: `"agency owner" OR "lead generation" OR "outbound" OR "growth agency"`

**Setup pattern:**
- Sender: your own profile or senior team member — agency buyers respond to founder-to-founder
- Message: reference the specific signal. Speak to the operational problem (account limits, ban risk, client management). Lead with the constraint they're feeling
- Follow-ups: 2–3, each adds a new operational angle (multi-client management, white-label capability, zero-ban track record)
- Chat flow: ENGAGE by acknowledging the operational challenge → IDENTIFY current setup and scale → PITCH infrastructure as solution → OFFER call or live demo of system running
- Smart FU: went quiet after interest, didn't book call, asked about pricing but didn't follow through

---

### 2. Direct Outreach to Agency Owners and Sales Leaders
Build a targeted list of agency owners using Sales Navigator. Direct, operationally-specific message.

| Metric | Benchmark |
|--------|-----------|
| CR acceptance | 18–25% |
| Reply rate | 8–15% |
| Hot leads per 100 delivered | 6–10 |
| Volume (10 profiles) | 2,000 connection requests/week |

**Sales Navigator search:**
```
Job Title:    "Agency Owner" OR "Founder" OR "CEO" OR "Head of Growth" OR
              "Head of Sales" OR "Sales Operations" OR "Lead Generation"
Company size: 1–10 OR 11–50
Industry:     Marketing & Advertising OR Internet OR Business Consulting OR Staffing
Geography:    United States OR United Kingdom OR Australia
Activity:     Posted on LinkedIn: Last 30 days
```

**Setup pattern:**
- Sender: 5–10 Fractional SDR profiles, SBL auto-rotates
- Message: direct, operational, under 250 characters. Speak to scale and safety. No "we help agencies grow" language
- A/B test: account safety angle vs multi-client scale angle vs white-label capability. Kill after 200 delivers
- Follow-ups: first as voice note (direct); second with different operational angle
- Chat flow: ENGAGE → IDENTIFY current client volume and setup → PITCH infrastructure that scales without risk → OFFER demo or trial

---

### 3. Retargeting Warm Leads and Existing Contacts
Re-engage people who showed interest but didn't convert — connected, asked questions, replied once then went quiet.

| Metric | Benchmark |
|--------|-----------|
| Re-engagement rate | 15–25% |
| Hot lead rate | 10–18% |
| Conversion to call | 8–15% |
| Time to convert | 3–7 days |

**Lead sources:**
- Existing campaign leads: filter to connected but didn't book, or replied positively then went quiet
- Pricing conversations: export contacts who discussed pricing → re-approach with free audit or case study
- Current clients: for upsell/expansion — new feature, expanded capacity, relevant case study

**Setup pattern:**
- Sender: your own profile — warm retargeting is personal
- Message: do NOT re-pitch. Reference the previous conversation or a new development. Keep short and personal
- Follow-ups: 1–2 maximum (warm leads — over-following kills goodwill)
- Chat flow: ENGAGE by acknowledging previous conversation → OFFER something new → PITCH only if they re-engage → clear low-friction next step

---

## Message guidelines
- Under 250 characters
- Speak the language of operations: account limits, ban risk, client campaign management, white-label
- Agency buyers have seen every pitch — sound like a peer, not a vendor
- Never "scale your outreach", "grow your pipeline", "AI-powered"
- One concrete operational constraint > three feature bullets

---

## Dashboard benchmarks to watch
| Signal | Healthy | Action needed |
|--------|---------|---------------|
| CR acceptance | 20%+ | < 15%: sounds like vendor pitch, switch to signal-based |
| Reply rate | 10%+ | < 8%: too long or too feature-focused, cut to 250 chars |
| Friction (red) | < 10% | > 15%: wrong segment (hitting in-house sales, not agencies) |
| Disengaged (yellow) | < 35% | > 40%: follow-up needs operational proof (case study, data point) |

Note: high objection rate (15–20%) is NOT bad for agencies. Agency buyers push back as part of evaluation. Watch objection + disengaged TOGETHER — that combination signals a problem.

---

## Fixing what's not working
- **Low acceptance (< 15%):** Sounds like vendor pitch. Switch to signal-based opener. Tighten ICP Filter — hitting in-house sales roles instead of agency owners?
- **Low reply rate (< 8%):** Lead with operational problem not solution. Cut to under 250 chars.
- **High friction (> 15%):** Wrong people — tighten to agency-specific industries and company sizes.
- **High disengaged (> 40%):** Follow-up should be operational proof (case study, specific data point). Add voice note as final FU.

---

## Scaling what works
1. After 200–300 delivers, confirm winner
2. Add Fractional SDR profiles to winning campaign
3. Use winning structure as template for each new client vertical — adjust ICP, message, chat flow objective, keep proven structure
4. Every 2 weeks: review sentiment, adjust chat flow for the specific objections showing up most
