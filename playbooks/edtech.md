# SBL Playbook — Edtech & Coaching

*35,000 delivered messages · 65 campaigns*

## Who this is for
Course creators, cohort founders, bootcamp operators, coaching businesses. Targeting working professionals, career switchers, and aspiring learners.

---

## Core insight
Edtech has the highest CR acceptance of any vertical — 53.4%. But 0.8% hot lead rate means acceptance alone doesn't convert. The gap between acceptance and conversion is where most edtech outreach fails. Lead with transformation (career outcome, skill, result) not the program.

---

## Campaign types (ranked by quality)

### 1. Warm Audience Outreach
Reach people who already have a relationship with your brand — webinar attendees, waitlist signups, past students, WhatsApp community members.

| Metric | Benchmark |
|--------|-----------|
| CR acceptance | 50–70% |
| Reply rate | 15–25% |
| Hot leads per 100 delivered | 8–15 |
| AI convo initiation | Very high |

**Lead sources:**
- CSV upload: webinar registrants who didn't attend, waitlist signups, past students
- WhatsApp: for audiences already in your ecosystem (no monthly limit in SBL)
- Existing campaign leads: retarget from previous campaigns who didn't convert

**Setup pattern:**
- Sender: your profile or a mentor/advisor persona — never a sales rep
- Message: reference exactly why you're reaching out (the action they took). Ask about their journey, don't pitch immediately
- Chat flow: ENGAGE by acknowledging the action → IDENTIFY their specific challenge → PITCH program as the path → OFFER next step (call, free session, enrolment link)
- Smart FU: 2–3, each adds something new (student success story, free resource, cohort deadline)

---

### 2. Post Engagement and Community Signal
Target people who liked/commented on content about career growth, skill-building, or the topic your course covers.

| Metric | Benchmark |
|--------|-----------|
| CR acceptance | 40–53% |
| Reply rate | 12–20% |
| Positive sentiment | 70–87% |
| Hot leads per 100 delivered | 6–10 |
| Comment-to-DM conversion | 15–25% |

**Lead sources:**
- Post Likes: posts about career transitions into your topic, certifications, skills gap
- Comment to DM: people who comment on career advice posts are actively looking
- Community signal: alumni networks, career development groups, interest groups
- Competitor posts: people engaging with competitor course or bootcamp content

**Setup pattern:**
- Message: reference the specific post or community. Lead with curiosity — not a pitch. Ask about their goal
- Follow-ups: 2–3 voice notes (student success story, free resource, cohort deadline)
- Chat flow: ENGAGE referencing their signal → IDENTIFY where they are and what's holding them back → PITCH your program as the specific solution → OFFER low-friction next step

---

### 3. Sales Navigator + ICP Filter (Cold at Scale)
Broadest reach. Works best as top-of-funnel → webinars/free resources, not direct enrolment.

| Metric | Benchmark |
|--------|-----------|
| CR acceptance | 20–35% |
| Reply rate | 8–15% |
| Hot leads per 100 delivered | 3–6 |
| Volume (10 profiles) | 2,000 connection requests/week |

**Sales Navigator search:**
```
Job Title:    [Role your course serves — e.g. "Software Engineer" OR "Data Analyst"]
Industry:     [Industry your learners come from]
Geography:    India / US / UK (wherever your learner base is)
Seniority:    Entry OR Associate OR Mid-Senior
Years in role: 1–5 years  ← people early enough to switch or upskill
Activity:     Posted on LinkedIn: Last 30 days
```

**Setup pattern:**
- Message: don't lead with the course — lead with the outcome or problem. Under 250 chars. Curiosity-driven
- A/B test: outcome-led, problem-led, social proof-led. Kill after 200 delivers per version
- Follow-ups: first as voice note (warm, personal); second with free resource or webinar invite
- Chat flow: ENGAGE → IDENTIFY current role + goal → PITCH program as bridge → OFFER free resource or webinar (lower friction than direct enrolment for cold)

---

## Message guidelines
- Lead with transformation, not features: "Get into product management" not "12-week course on PM skills"
- Under 250 characters — edtech audiences skim
- No "limited seats", "last chance", "enrol now" in openers
- Ask about their goal, not your program
- Persona must feel like a helpful mentor, not a sales rep

---

## Dashboard benchmarks to watch
| Signal | Healthy | Action needed |
|--------|---------|---------------|
| CR acceptance | 30%+ | < 20%: opener sounds like a pitch |
| Reply rate | 10%+ | < 8%: lead with question about goal, not course statement |
| Friction (red) | < 10% | > 15%: remove urgency language, check ICP (career stage) |
| Disengaged (yellow) | < 35% | > 40%: follow-up should feel helpful, not push again |

---

## Fixing what's not working
- **Low acceptance (< 20%):** Switch to warm audience before going cold. Reference a specific signal.
- **Low reply rate (< 8%):** Lead with question about their goal. Cut to under 250 chars.
- **High friction (> 15%):** Remove urgency language. Check ICP — wrong career stage?
- **High disengaged (> 40%):** Follow-up should offer a free resource or student story, not re-pitch. Add voice note as final FU.

---

## Scaling what works
1. After 200–300 delivers, confirm winner
2. Add Fractional SDR profiles to winning campaign
3. Use best campaign as top-of-funnel for a webinar → run retargeting campaign to attendees who didn't enrol
4. Every 2 weeks: review sentiment, adjust for objections and drop-off points showing up most
