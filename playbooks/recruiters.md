# SBL Playbook — Recruiters & HR Teams

*32,000+ delivered messages · 113 campaigns*

## Who this is for
Recruitment agencies, in-house talent teams, HR consultancies. Running outreach on two sides simultaneously: candidates who fit open roles, and hiring managers who need talent.

---

## Core insight
Recruitment is the only vertical with two parallel outreach directions. 28.7% hot lead rate on the hiring manager side — second highest of any ICP segment. Reason: hiring is urgent. A hiring manager with an open role responds immediately to a specialist who can solve it.

53%+ CR acceptance on the candidate side, but acceptance alone doesn't mean placement — conversation quality is what converts.

**Run candidate outreach and hiring manager outreach as completely separate campaigns. Different personas, different messages, different chat flows.**

---

## Campaign types

### 1. Candidate Outreach (Signal-Based)
Identify candidates who fit an open role using behavioral signals — not a cold blast.

| Metric | Benchmark |
|--------|-----------|
| CR acceptance | 50–65% |
| Reply rate | 15–25% |
| Qualified candidates per 100 delivered | 8–15 |
| Avg conversation depth | 4–7 messages |

**Lead sources:**
- Post likes and comment signals: posts about career transitions, job searching, skill development
- Community and alumni signal: professional associations, alumni networks where target candidates gather
- CSV from ATS/CRM: warmest audience — already engaged with your firm
- WhatsApp: for warm candidate nurturing, voice notes perform best
- Sales Navigator search:
```
Job Title:    [Current title your candidate holds]
Seniority:    Entry OR Associate OR Mid-Senior
Years in role: 2–5 years  ← sweet spot for openness to new opportunities
Activity:     Posted on LinkedIn: Last 30 days
```

**Setup pattern:**
- Sender: recruiter profile that feels like a career advisor — warm, knowledgeable, not a sales rep
- Message: reference their current role or the signal. Express genuine interest in their career goals. Under 250 characters
- Chat flow: ENGAGE by acknowledging their background → IDENTIFY career goals and current situation → QUALIFY for the specific role → HAND OFF to human recruiter when qualified
- Smart FU: warm check-in, specific opportunity or resource, final gentle nudge

---

### 2. Hiring Manager and Company Outreach
Reach decision-makers at companies who need talent: HR leaders, hiring managers, department heads, founders.

| Metric | Benchmark |
|--------|-----------|
| CR acceptance | 22–35% |
| Reply rate | 10–18% |
| Hot leads per 100 delivered | 8–12 |
| Avg conversation depth | 3–5 messages |

**Lead sources:**
- Job posting signal (highest intent): companies that recently posted roles you can fill — identify the hiring manager
- Post likes: hiring managers engaging with content about talent acquisition challenges, team scaling
- Comment to DM: posts about hiring difficulties, talent shortages — anyone commenting is openly discussing their problem
- Sales Navigator:
```
Job Title:    "HR Manager" OR "Talent Acquisition" OR "Head of HR" OR
              "Hiring Manager" OR "VP People" OR "Founder" OR "CEO"
Company size: 11–50 OR 51–200 OR 201–500
Industry:     [Industry you recruit for]
Activity:     Posted on LinkedIn: Last 30 days
```

**Setup pattern:**
- Sender: your own profile or senior recruiter — hiring managers respond to specialist-to-specialist
- Message: reference the hiring signal if you have one (recent job posting, post they liked). Lead with the specific talent problem you solve. Under 250 chars
- A/B test: referencing job posting vs post they liked vs cold with specific placement claim
- Follow-ups: first as voice note (direct, specific about what you can place); second with speed of placement, niche specialization, or relevant placement case
- Chat flow: ENGAGE referencing hiring signal → IDENTIFY specific role or team they're filling → PITCH your placement capability → OFFER call to discuss available candidates

---

### 3. Retargeting and Re-Engagement
Re-engage warm contacts from both sides: candidates who didn't place, hiring managers you spoke to but didn't close. Hiring needs change — the manager not hiring 3 months ago may have 5 open roles now.

| Metric | Benchmark |
|--------|-----------|
| Re-engagement rate | 20–35% |
| Hot lead rate | 12–20% |
| Conversion to placement discussion | 10–18% |
| Time to re-engage | 3–7 days |

**Setup pattern:**
- Message: reference previous interaction or a new development (new role, market update, specific opportunity matching what they told you). Never re-pitch generically
- Follow-ups: 1–2 maximum
- Chat flow: ENGAGE by acknowledging previous relationship → OFFER something new → QUALIFY again if situation changed → HAND OFF when qualified

---

## Message guidelines
- **Candidate campaigns:** Lead with career outcome not job spec. Reference signal or shared context. Sound like a trusted career advisor
- **Hiring manager campaigns:** Lead with specific talent problem you solve, not agency credentials. Reference job posting or engagement signal if you have one
- Under 250 characters for both
- Persona distinction is critical: career advisor (candidate side), business partner (hiring side)

---

## Fixing what's not working
- **Low candidate acceptance (< 30%):** Reference specific signal or shared context. Persona must feel like career advisor not recruiter running a blast.
- **Low hiring manager acceptance (< 15%):** Lead with talent problem not credentials. Check ICP — hitting coordinators instead of decision-makers?
- **High disengaged (> 40%):** For candidates: helpful career check-in not another recruitment push. Voice note as final FU.
- **Low reply rate (< 10%):** For candidates: career outcome not job spec. For hiring: specific placement claim not generic recruitment language.

---

## Scaling what works
1. Run candidate and hiring manager campaigns in parallel — the two pipelines feed each other
2. Use best candidate campaign as template for each new role — adjust job title filter and ICP, keep proven structure
3. Use best hiring manager campaign as template for each new industry vertical you enter
4. Review sentiment separately for candidate and hiring manager campaigns — they behave differently and need separate optimization
5. Every 2 weeks: check both sentiment distributions independently
